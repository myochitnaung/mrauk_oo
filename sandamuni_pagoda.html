Sanda Muni Pagoda

The Sandamuni statue is a statue cast by King Thuriya Sakka during the Third Dynasty.

Location

The Sandamuni statue is located in the southwestern part of Mrauk-U, Rakhine State , and is now housed in the former Sandamuni Pagoda, formerly known as Bandula Temple.


Dimensions

  • Wisdom 4 feet 10 inches
  • 4 feet 8 inches wide on both knees
  • Waist circumference 4 feet 1 inch
  • Arm circumference 1 foot 11 inches
  • Shoulders 2 feet 10 inches wide
  • 5 feet 10 inches around the chest
    • The Sandamuni statue is made of a mixture of five metals called Pyinsaloha. Proportionately mixed to enjoy the rays.


Shape

The statue has a face down and eyes are cast in the eyes. His face was round and his forehead was covered with no bandage. The long ears and wide ears are a Rakhine style. The neck is straight and has three trims. Because of the thin lotus robe, I can see her breasts. The arm is shaped like an elephant. It has a waist that fits over the chest. The statue rests on its left foot and its right foot. The right forearm is raised and placed on the right foot. His right forearm is down, but it does not touch the throne. His fingers are as short and long as normal. The left palm is turned over and placed on the right palm. He holds the fire of hell between his thumb and forefinger.


History

During the Dhvad period, King Thuriya Sakka, after the casting of the Sandathuriya King Mahamuni Buddha, took the excess jewels and cast five statues. Sandamuni, Sakyamuni, Devimuni·Åä Rajamuni They are called Sulamuni. They are made of Mahamuni and the rest of the jewels and are called Mahayana statues. King Thuriya Sakka erected the five-headed statue in a suitable place near the Mahamuni Pagoda, and the Sandamuni statue was also located on the hill of Dipapabbata near Taung Oo village. From the Dhavadi period to the Vesali period; From the Vesali period to the Lay Myo era; From the Lay Myo period to the Mrauk-U period, Min Saw Mon brought the Sandamuni statue to Mrauk-U and built a pagoda on Mount Barbu.
